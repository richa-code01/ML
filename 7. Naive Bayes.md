### 3. Naive Bayes Algorithm

A probability-based classification algorithm based on **Bayes Theorem**.

Used mainly for:
- Spam Detection
- Sentiment Analysis
- Text Classification

---

### Independent Events

Events that do not affect each other.

#### Dice Example

$$
P(6)=\frac{1}{6}
$$

#### Coin Toss

$$
P(Head)=P(Tail)=\frac{1}{2}
$$

Result of one event does not affect another.

#### Formula

$$
P(A \cap B)=P(A)\times P(B)
$$

---

### Dependent Events

One event affects another event.

#### Deck of Cards Example

- Total cards = 52
- Red cards = 26
- Black cards = 26

#### First Draw

$$
P(Red)=\frac{26}{52}=\frac{1}{2}
$$

#### Second Draw (without replacement)

After removing one red card:

- Remaining red cards = 25
- Remaining total cards = 51

$$
P(Red\ in\ second\ draw)=\frac{25}{51}
$$

So probabilities change.

#### Formula

$$
P(A \cap B)=P(A)\times P(B|A)
$$

where:

$$
P(B|A)
$$

means:
- probability of `B`
- given `A` already happened

This is called **Conditional Probability**.

---

### Bayes Theorem Derivation

We know:

$$
P(A \cap B)=P(B \cap A)
$$

Using conditional probability:

$$
P(A)\times P(B|A)=P(B)\times P(A|B)
$$

Rearranging:

$$
P(A|B)=\frac{P(A)\times P(B|A)}{P(B)}
$$

This is **Bayes Theorem**.

---

### Naive Bayes Assumption

Input features are assumed to be:
- independent of each other

Output depends on input features.

---

### Naive Bayes Formula

For features:

$$
X=(x_1,x_2,x_3,...,x_n)
$$

Bayes theorem becomes:

$$
P(Y|X)=\frac{P(Y)\times P(X|Y)}{P(X)}
$$

Expanding:

$$
P(Y|x_1,x_2,...,x_n)=\frac{P(Y)\times P(x_1|Y)\times P(x_2|Y)\times ... \times P(x_n|Y)}{P(x_1)\times P(x_2)\times ... \times P(x_n)}
$$

---

### Classification

For binary classification:

#### YES Class

$$
P(Yes|X)=\frac{P(Yes)\times P(x_1|Yes)\times P(x_2|Yes)\times ...}{P(x_1)\times P(x_2)\times ...}
$$

#### NO Class

$$
P(No|X)=\frac{P(No)\times P(x_1|No)\times P(x_2|No)\times ...}{P(x_1)\times P(x_2)\times ...}
$$

---

### Final Prediction

Whichever probability is higher:
- classified as final output

Example:

```text
P(Yes|X) > P(No|X)
```

Prediction:

```text
YES
```

---

### Why "Naive"?

Because algorithm assumes:
- all input features are independent

which may not always be true in real life.

---

### Advantages

- Simple and fast
- Works well on text data
- Performs well on small datasets

---

### Disadvantages

- Assumes feature independence
- Less effective for highly correlated features
